A person regularly traveling between the Bay Area, Sacramento, Las Vegas, Portland, and Seattle likely works in one of these roles:

Regional Sales Manager or Business Development - Companies with West Coast operations often divide territories to cover these major metro areas, requiring regular client visits and relationship building.

Field Auditor or Compliance Inspector - Government agencies (federal/state) or large corporations need auditors to visit multiple office locations or facilities across these cities.

Regional Account Executive - Tech companies, healthcare suppliers, or other B2B businesses frequently have reps covering the Pacific Northwest and Northern California/Nevada territories.

Labor Union Representative or Organizer - Many unions have jurisdictions covering multiple states and require regular travel to different local chapters.

Regional Trainer or Implementation Specialist - Companies rolling out new software, equipment, or procedures often send specialists to train staff at different branch locations.

Circuit Court Attorney or Judge - Federal courts have circuits covering multiple states, though this is less common for this specific cluster of cities.

Regional Operations Manager - Retail chains, logistics companies, or hospitality groups with locations in these cities need managers overseeing multiple sites.

The connecting thread is that these cities form a logical West Coast regional territory covering Northern California, Nevada, Oregon, and Washington - a common business division for companies with multi-state operations.

Claude 2025-10-13 03:14

Most of these roles would pay over $100k/year, especially with the regular travel component and West Coast cost of living adjustments. Here are the strongest candidates:

Regional Sales Manager/Business Development - Typically $120k-200k+ with commission/bonuses, especially in tech, pharmaceuticals, or industrial B2B sales. High performers can earn significantly more.

Regional Account Executive (Enterprise/Tech) - $150k-250k+ total compensation in tech hubs like the Bay Area and Seattle. Base salaries often $100k-140k before commissions.

Regional Operations Manager - $110k-180k depending on industry and company size. Logistics, retail chains, and hospitality groups typically pay well for multi-site oversight.

Field Auditor (Senior/Specialized) - $100k-150k+ for experienced auditors, especially in financial services, healthcare compliance, or government (GS-13/14 levels for federal positions).

Regional Trainer/Implementation Specialist (Enterprise Software) - $100k-160k, particularly for specialized technical systems or enterprise platforms.

Circuit Court positions - Federal judges earn $200k+, and senior attorneys in federal practice easily exceed $100k.

Less likely to consistently hit $100k:

  • Union organizers (though senior representatives might reach this level)
  • Entry-level positions in any of these fields

Given the Bay Area and Seattle markets specifically, nearly all mid-to-senior level positions involving regional travel responsibility would need to pay $100k+ to attract qualified candidates.
