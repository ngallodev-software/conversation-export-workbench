# Solution as an Experienced Physicist

Written Explanation

This is a classic impulse-momentum problem involving a collision between a billiard ball and a cushion. The key principle here is the impulse-momentum theorem, which states that the impulse (force multiplied by time) equals the change in momentum.

When the ball strikes the cushion perpendicularly and rebounds, it experiences a change in velocity from +2.1 m/s to -2.0 m/s. The positive and negative signs indicate opposite directions - the ball approaches in one direction and rebounds in the opposite direction.

The cushion exerts a force on the ball during the contact time, causing this momentum change. Since we know the mass, the velocity change, and the contact time, we can calculate the average force exerted by the cushion.

Important Note: I'm interpreting the contact time as 3.3 × 10⁻³ seconds (0.0033 seconds), which is physically reasonable for a ball-cushion collision. If you meant 3.3 × 10³ seconds (3300 seconds), that would be nearly an hour of contact, which wouldn't make physical sense for this scenario.

Mathematical Solution

Given:

  • Mass: m = 0.38 kg
  • Initial velocity: v₁ = +2.1 m/s
  • Final velocity: v₂ = -2.0 m/s
  • Contact time: Δt = 3.3 × 10⁻³ s = 0.0033 s

Find: Average net force F̄

Solution:

Using the impulse-momentum theorem: $$F̄ \cdot \Delta t = \Delta p = m(v2 - v1)$$

Step 1: Calculate the change in momentum $$\Delta p = m(v2 - v1)$$ $$\Delta p = 0.38 \text{ kg} \times (-2.0 - 2.1) \text{ m/s}$$ $$\Delta p = 0.38 \times (-4.1)$$ $$\Delta p = -1.558 \text{ kg·m/s}$$

Step 2: Calculate the average force $$F̄ = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t}$$ $$F̄ = \frac{-1.558 \text{ kg·m/s}}{3.3 \times 10^{-3} \text{ s}}$$ $$F̄ = \frac{-1.558}{0.0033}$$ $$F̄ = -472.1 \text{ N}$$

Answer:

  • Magnitude: |F̄| = 472 N (or 470 N with appropriate significant figures)
  • Direction: The negative sign indicates the force is directed opposite to the initial velocity (i.e., the cushion pushes back on the ball)

Now, as a High School Physics Tutor...

Hey! Let me explain this problem in a simpler way.

What's happening here? Imagine you're throwing a tennis ball at a wall. The ball hits the wall and bounces back, right? That's exactly what's happening with this billiard ball and the cushion!

The Big Idea: When the ball hits the cushion, its speed changes - it was going one direction at 2.1 m/s, and now it's going the opposite direction at 2.0 m/s. Something must have pushed it to change direction, and that "something" is the cushion pushing back on the ball.

The Math (don't worry, it's straightforward!):

Think of it in steps:

  1. How much did the velocity change?

- Started at: +2.1 m/s (moving toward the cushion) - Ended at: -2.0 m/s (moving away from the cushion) - Change = -2.0 - (+2.1) = -4.1 m/s

That negative sign? It just means the velocity changed in the opposite direction!

  1. How much did the momentum change?

- Momentum is just mass × velocity - Change in momentum = 0.38 kg × (-4.1 m/s) = -1.558 kg·m/s

  1. What force caused this change?

- We use this formula: Force = (Change in momentum) ÷ (Time) - Force = -1.558 ÷ 0.0033 = -472 Newtons

What does -472 N mean?

  • The 472 tells us HOW STRONG the push was - that's pretty strong! (For comparison, lifting a gallon of milk takes about 40 N)
  • The negative sign tells us the DIRECTION - the cushion pushed opposite to the ball's original motion (which makes sense - it's pushing the ball back!)

The Bottom Line: The cushion exerts a force of 472 N pushing the ball back in the opposite direction from which it came. That's what causes the ball to rebound!
