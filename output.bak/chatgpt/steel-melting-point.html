The temperature at which office furniture and similar materials burn depends on the type of material. Here are general estimates:

Wood (e.g., desks, chairs):

  • Ignition temperature: ~390°F to 570°F (200°C to 300°C)
  • Once ignited, it burns at temperatures of 1,100°F to 1,800°F (593°C to 982°C).

Plastics (e.g., chairs, laminates):

  • Ignition temperature: ~600°F to 800°F (315°C to 427°C) for common plastics.
  • Burning plastics can reach temperatures of 1,300°F to 2,200°F (704°C to 1,204°C).

Fabric (e.g., cushions, upholstery):

  • Ignition temperature: ~400°F to 480°F (200°C to 250°C), depending on the type of fabric.
  • Burning fabric can sustain temperatures of 1,100°F to 1,600°F (593°C to 871°C).

These materials also release significant heat and toxic smoke during combustion, especially synthetics. Fire safety regulations often require flame-retardant treatments for office furniture to slow down ignition.
